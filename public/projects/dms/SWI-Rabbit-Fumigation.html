SWI — Rabbit Warren Fumigation (Phostoxin)

NATSOP RAB004 aligned — S7 Authorised Operators Only
Document: TMC-SWI-RAB-FUM  |  v1.0  |  20 July 2026
Scope: Diffusion fumigation of rabbit warrens using Aluminium Phosphide (Phostoxin) on DMS land. Area 21 & Barry's Block.

Key Controls

  • Only S7 authorised operators may handle and apply Phostoxin.
  • Complete risk assessment and Pre-Start JSB before any fumigation event.
  • Set and sign exclusion zones — do not enter until cleared.
  • Collect and remove all carcasses to avoid secondary poisoning of native predators.

Step-by-step Protocol

  1. Survey: Map all warren entrances, record GPS, classify active/inactive.
  2. Brief: Crew brief on exclusion zone, wind, PPE, emergency response.
  3. Apply: Place tablets per NATSOP RAB004 instructions, seal entrances as required.
  4. Exclude: Establish exclusion zone signage and barriers for minimum 48–72 hours.
  5. Return: After safe period, return with PPE to collect carcasses and record disposal.
  6. Report: Submit fumigation event record in Fulcrum (warren locations, tablet counts, operator S7 confirmation, carcass count).

PPE & Monitoring

Minimum PPE: Full-face respirator with appropriate cartridge, chemical-resistant overalls, gloves, boots. Continuous monitoring of wind direction. If any human exposure occurs, call 000 and Poisons Info 13 11 26.

Emergency

Phosphine is acutely toxic. If exposure suspected, evacuate area and seek emergency medical assistance immediately. Notify DMS and TMC management.

Don’t Proceed If

  1. Operator S7 authorisation cannot be verified.
  2. Wind direction or speed suggests unsafe gas movement toward workers or public.
  3. No exclusion signage or barriers available.
  4. Weather forecasts show heavy rain that could compromise fumigation containment.

Records

Use Fulcrum: record warren mapping, active/inactive, tablet counts, operator name and S7 details, carcass collection evidence (photos, GPS), date/time stamps.
