⚠ HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK — MANDATORY SWMS

This work is classified as High Risk Construction Work (HRCW) under Occupational Health and Safety Regulations 2017 — Regulation 5.1.13: Work carried out adjacent to a road used by traffic. A SWMS is mandatory before works commence. All workers must sign this SWMS before each shift. This document must be available on site at all times. Do not commence work if conditions differ from those described — review and re-brief crew first.

3. Work Steps — Hazard Identification, Risk Assessment & Controls

Risk Matrix: Likelihood (1–5) × Consequence (1–5) = Risk Rating  |  Low: 1–4  |  Medium: 5–9  |  High: 10–14  |  Extreme: 15–25  |  Residual risk ≥ HIGH requires Supervisor sign-off before proceeding.
# Work Step Hazards Initial Risk Controls — Hierarchy of Control Residual Risk Responsible
1 Site Establishment & Mobilisation Vehicle hazards, unloading injuries, site unfamiliarity, unsecured loads MED (8)
  • Pre-start JSB in Fulcrum before travel
  • Site-specific induction for all new personnel before access
  • Vehicle pre-start checks in Fulcrum Equipment Hub
  • All loads secured, no loose items in tray
  • Compound area away from live lanes
 LOW (4) Supervisor
2 Traffic Management Setup
Deploy TGS TA052611 A/B/C, TMA, signs, cones per MOA-0091800		 Live 110 km/h freeway traffic during TM setup, vehicle intrusion, TM sequence gap, worker strike EXTREME (20)
  • ELIMINATE: No personnel on carriageway until TGS fully deployed and TC confirms safe
  • ENGINEERING: TMA deployed FIRST — terminal protection before any other device
  • Activate MOA-0091800 via RAPP or TOC 1300 107 757 before any TCDs deployed
  • Check VicTraffic before each deployment
  • Certified Traffic Controller leads all TM — no substitution
  • All crew hi-vis Level 2 fluorescent mandatory
  • Mon–Fri 07:00–17:00 only (MOA condition)
  • TGS sequence: advance signs → taper → cones → arrow board → TMA
  • Radio comms confirmed before setup commences
 MED (8) TC + Supervisor
3 Fence Line Clearing & Preparation
Remove debris, old mesh, damaged posts		 Wire/mesh cuts, snake/wildlife encounter, dust, manual handling strain, wire end eye strike HIGH (12)
  • Cut-resistant gloves Level D mandatory — all wire/mesh handling
  • Safety glasses mandatory — wire fragment hazards
  • Probe all vegetation before putting hands in — snake awareness
  • Team lifts for heavy items, mechanical assist where available
  • Minimum clearing footprint — fence line only
  • All cleared material removed same day
 LOW (4) Field crew
4 Post Installation
Drive galvanised steel posts at 3.33 m centres using post driver		 Plant-person interaction, noise, overhead powerlines, vibration, rollover on slope HIGH (15)
  • ELIMINATE: Confirm no overhead powerlines before driving in each section (SWI-1001)
  • 5 m exclusion zone around post driver during operation
  • Current VOC confirmed for all plant operators
  • Hearing protection mandatory within 5 m of post driver
  • Slope stability assessed before driving on any embankment
  • Operator in constant visual/radio contact with spotter
  • Daily plant pre-start in Fulcrum before operation
 MED (6) Plant Op + Supervisor
5 Post Surface Prep & Cold Galvanising
Grind post surfaces, apply Dulux Metalshield Cold Galv Primer (SWI-107)		 Chemical skin/eye exposure (solvent-based), fire/ignition risk, grinding dust, waste disposal MED (9)
  • SDS on site — reviewed at pre-start briefing
  • Solvent-resistant gloves + splash goggles mandatory during application
  • No ignition sources within 3 m of primer — no smoking
  • Apply in ventilated area only — no confined spaces
  • No application near drains, waterways or Barkers Creek
  • Waste rags/containers in sealed waste bin — no ground disposal
  • Fire extinguisher within reach during application
 LOW (4) Field crew + Supervisor
6 Mesh Installation
Unroll 315/50 wildlife mesh, tension to posts, tie-off at 150 mm centres, 5 helicoil support wires		 Laceration from wire ends, spring-back injury during tensioning, manual handling (rolls 40–60 kg), eye strike HIGH (12)
  • Minimum 2 persons for all mesh unrolling — one each end, controlled unroll
  • Cut-resistant gloves Level D mandatory — all mesh handling, no exceptions
  • Safety glasses mandatory throughout mesh work
  • Mesh rolls transported on vehicle carrier — no drag unrolling
  • Team lifts for rolls over 20 kg — no single-person lift of full roll
  • Stand aside from spring-back path during tensioning
  • Section 707 tie-off spacing followed per SWI-104
  • ITP hold point photos in Fulcrum before proceeding
 LOW (4) Field crew + Supervisor
7 Floppy-Top Overhang Fitting
Attach floppy-top panel to top of 2.4 m fence		 Working at height (2.4 m), balance hazard, ladder instability, mesh laceration at height MED (9)
  • No standing on fence rail or posts at any time
  • Rated step ladder — minimum 120 kg rating, footed on firm ground
  • 3-point contact on ladder at all times
  • Cut-resistant gloves mandatory throughout
  • Supervisor verifies floppy-top angle per Section 707 at each post before moving on
  • Photo evidence in Fulcrum for each completed section
 LOW (4) Field crew
8 Ground Skirt / Apron Installation
Lay mesh apron, peg and secure per Section 707		 Manual handling, snake/fauna encounter at ground level, mesh cuts, drainage obstruction MED (8)
  • Cut-resistant gloves mandatory for all ground-level mesh work
  • Probe vegetation before putting hands to ground — slow approach
  • Any apron gap or incomplete section noted as ITP Hold Point in Fulcrum before moving on
  • Check apron does not obstruct culverts or drainage paths (SWI-106)
 LOW (4) Field crew
9 Erosion Control — Sites 1 & 3
Install coir matting and coir logs per DSE26032REP01		 Manual handling, steep slope access, working near drainage swales, outflanking gaps if incorrectly installed HIGH (10)
  • Slope stability assessed by Supervisor before work commences
  • 3-point contact on all slope traversing
  • Coir matting: top of bank to top of bank, pegged at max 1 m intervals
  • Coir logs 150–200 mm dia: span full channel width, sufficient length to prevent outflanking
  • Install per DSE26032REP01 and manufacturer specifications
  • STOP WORK — Site 2 (Barkers Creek): NO works until North Central CMA Waterway Permit obtained + TMC PM written authorisation
  • Site 3 (freeway-side): MOA-0091800 covers access — TM required before works
 LOW (4) Supervisor
10 QA Inspection & ITP Hold Points
Before / hold point / after photos, Section 707 compliance, Supervisor ITP sign-off		 Non-conformance if QA bypassed, incomplete records, section signed off but non-compliant MED (8)
  • Before photos in Fulcrum before works commence on each section
  • Hold point photos during installation — mesh tension, tie-off spacing, post spacing
  • After/completion photos — full section, apron, floppy-top confirmed
  • Supervisor ITP sign-off in Fulcrum before section marked complete
  • Material compliance (Section 707) confirmed and recorded per SWI-104
  • NCR raised if defect found — do not proceed until resolved
 LOW (4) Supervisor
11 TM Demobilisation & Site Cleanup
Remove all TCDs in reverse order, deactivate MOA, clear site		 Live traffic re-exposure during TM removal, unsecured materials on carriageway, incomplete site clearance EXTREME (20)
  • Remove TCDs in strict reverse order of installation — per TGS protocol
  • All personnel clear of carriageway before TMA relocated
  • Deactivate MOA-0091800 via RAPP or TOC 1300 107 757 after ALL devices removed and site clear
  • Full site sweep by Supervisor before demob confirmed complete
  • Zero waste, mesh or materials left on or near carriageway
 MED (6) TC + Supervisor

⚠ STOP WORK — DO NOT COMMENCE IF ANY OF THE FOLLOWING APPLY:

  • Pre-Start JSB not completed in Fulcrum for today's shift
  • MOA-0091800 not yet activated via RAPP or TOC (1300 107 757) — no TCDs to be deployed before activation
  • TGS TA052611 A/B/C not fully set up OR TMA not deployed
  • Certified Traffic Controller not on site and confirmed for this shift
  • Site conditions materially different from those described in this SWMS — re-brief all crew before proceeding
  • White Card or TMC Core 1 not confirmed for any worker on site
  • Any works at Site 2 Barkers Creek — Waterway Permit not yet obtained from North Central CMA
  • Section 707 material compliance not confirmed for the section being installed
  • Emergency contacts not confirmed with all crew at pre-start briefing
